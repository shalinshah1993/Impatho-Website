According to the WHO, 200,000 infants are born with sickle-cell in Africa every year, with Nigeria accounting for about three-quarters of these births. Sixty percent of the 200,000 will die as infants. Nigeria accounts for 75 percent of infant sickle-cell cases in Africa and almost 80 percent of infant deaths are because of this disease, in the continent. Sadiq Wali, president of the Nigeria Sickle-cell Foundation
